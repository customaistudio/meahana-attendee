ENGAGEMENT_SCORE (float, 1.0-10.0):
This measures the organizer's success at creating genuine participation - not just attendance, but contribution.

Key indicators of organizer-driven engagement:
- Voice balance: Did multiple people contribute substantively, or did 1-2 people dominate?
- Invitation: Did the organizer actively draw out quieter participants?
- Building: Did participants build on each other's ideas (sign of safety and interest)?
- Questions: Were people asking genuine questions or just listening passively?
- Energy: Did engagement sustain throughout or fade?

Scoring guide:
- 9-10: Everyone contributed meaningfully. The organizer created conditions where people wanted to participate.
- 7-8: Good participation with minor imbalances. Most people engaged.
- 5-6: Uneven. A few people carried the discussion. Others were present but passive.
- 3-4: Dominated by 1-2 voices or mostly monologue. Little genuine dialogue.
- 1-2: Essentially a one-way broadcast or people actively disengaged.

A meeting with 6 people where only 2 speak substantively is a 4-5, regardless of how good those 2 contributions were.
